Yuzu, a citrus fruit originating from East Asia, has found its way into the world of beer brewing due to its unique flavor profile. This fruit is known for its aromatic and tangy taste that combines elements of lemon, lime, and grapefruit. When Yuzu is added to beer during the brewing process, it imparts a refreshing and zesty character that enhances the overall taste experience.

The influence of Yuzu on beer can vary depending on how it is used. It can be incorporated as juice or zest directly into the brew or added during fermentation to infuse its flavors gradually. Yuzu adds brightness and complexity to beers by complementing hop bitterness with its own natural acidity. Additionally, this fruit brings out floral notes in certain styles while adding a distinctive citrus twist.

Brewers use Yuzu for various purposes, ranging from creating new experimental flavors to reinventing classic beer styles. Its properties lend well to both light-bodied lagers and bold IPAs alike. Yuzu's combination of tartness and sweetness makes it an excellent ingredient for sour beers such as gose or Berliner weisse where it contributes a vibrant citrus kick alongside lactobacillus bacteria's acidity. Moreover, Yuzu finds application in wheat beers like witbier or American pale ale variants where it adds a bright and crisp element that complements the smooth malt base.
