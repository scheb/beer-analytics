Caramel is a widely used ingredient in beer brewing that adds a sweet and toasty flavor profile to the final product. It influences the taste of beer by imparting rich, caramelized notes that can range from light and subtle to dark and intense, depending on the degree of caramelization. This addition brings depth and complexity to the overall flavor profile, creating a more balanced and enjoyable drinking experience.

In brewing, caramel is primarily used for its ability to enhance both color and aroma while adding sweetness. Its properties include providing body and mouthfeel, improving head retention, stabilizing foam structure, and contributing natural preservative properties. Caramel malts are commonly added during mashing or steeping in order to extract their flavors into the wort before fermentation begins.
