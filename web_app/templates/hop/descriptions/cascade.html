Cascade hops are a popular variety of American hop that were first introduced in the 1970s. They are known for their distinctive floral and citrus-like aroma, which makes them a favorite among brewers for use in a wide range of beer styles.

Cascade hops are a hybrid of English Fuggle and Russian Serebrianker hops, and were originally developed by the United States Department of Agriculture in Oregon. They have since become one of the most widely grown and used hop varieties in the US, particularly in the Pacific Northwest where they are predominantly grown.

The aroma of Cascade hops is characterized by its floral and spicy notes, with hints of grapefruit and other citrus fruits. These aromas make Cascade hops particularly well-suited for use in American-style pale ales, IPAs, and other hop-forward beers. Cascade hops also contribute a moderate level of bitterness, which can range from 4% to 7.5% alpha acid content, depending on the specific crop and growing conditions. This bitterness makes them a versatile hop for use in both bittering and flavoring stages of the brewing process.

Overall, Cascade hops are a beloved and versatile variety that have played a significant role in the development of American craft beer over the past few decades.
