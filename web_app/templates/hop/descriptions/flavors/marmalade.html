Marmalade flavor is a unique and complex characteristic, resulting from the combination of citrusy and jammy notes. This flavor profile is typically associated with certain hop varieties such as Amarillo, Citra, and Mosaic. It is highly desirable in beer styles like New England IPAs, American Pale Ales, and Double IPAs, where the fruity and juicy flavors complement the hoppy bitterness. The marmalade character adds a sweet and tangy dimension, balancing the taste while enhancing the overall drinking experience.
